Design Concept · Built from the Mobbin Research

This is what LittleLoop looks like when the research becomes screens.

Every mockup below is derived from a documented pattern in the competitive teardown — Netflix's kid grids, Disney+'s locked profiles, HBO Max's kid-proof exit, Instagram's break screen, YouTube's watch-time recap. Two worlds, one wall: a sunny child world in front, a calm parent world behind the PIN.

The LittleLoop product system — one brand, two temperatures

Type
Fredoka for joy, IBM Plex Sans for parent UI. Rounded display type reads friendly to kids; the plain sans keeps parent screens honest and data-legible.
Rules of the two worlds

Child: gradients, mascots, ≥64px touch targets, almost no text.
Parent: flat paper cards, one accent, numbers first.
The wall: every crossing goes through the PIN gate.

pin-gate
1
The gate explains its scope.

"Enter your PIN to leave Child Mode and open settings" — copied from HBO Max's pattern of stating exactly what the PIN does. No mystery, no fear.

From: HBO Max Kids Mode PIN
2
Biometrics as the fast lane.

Face ID / Touch ID sits right on the gate for the parent's daily use; the 4-digit PIN stays as the universal fallback.

LittleLoop spec + Netflix keypad pattern
3

Email-based reset. A locked-out parent is a churned parent — recovery is a first-class citizen, not a buried FAQ.

From: Netflix / HBO Max recovery links
＋
The owl keeps it friendly.

When a child inevitably taps the gate, they meet the wise owl saying "grown-ups only" — a boundary character, not an error state.

Principle: calm boundaries

Full-screen, calm, final.

The whole world shifts to sunset colors — unmissable but soothing. It's a scene change, not an interruption modal a child can dismiss.

From: Instagram "You've reached your daily limit"
2
Positive framing, tiny celebration.

"All done for today!" plus what they watched — ending on accomplishment, borrowing Instagram's "now's a good time" tone but pitched for a preschooler.

From: Instagram copy tone
3
No kid-accessible bypass. At all.

Where Instagram offers "Ignore limit for today," LittleLoop offers nothing the child can tap. Extra time is a parent decision behind the PIN — the boundary stays consistent, which is what prevents the meltdown.

Deliberate inversion of Instagram's escape hatch

player / fullscreen landscape
1
The embed is sandboxed visually.

YouTube provides the stream; LittleLoop provides every pixel of UI around it. No channel name, no comments, no share, no logo-as-link.

From: research finding — hide the chrome
2
Three controls a small hand can't miss.

Giant coral play/pause flanked by previous/next — skipping moves through the approved playlist only, matching Headspace's warmth with your existing control triad.

From: Headspace player + current LittleLoop build
3
"Up next" only ever means the playlist.

The up-next card shows the next parent-approved video — labeled as such — never a YouTube recommendation. The queue ends when the playlist does.

Safe inversion of YouTube autoplay · current build
4
Fullscreen keeps the same three controls.

Rotating to landscape changes nothing but size: identical triad, centered, over a tap-anywhere scrim. Controls fade after 3s; any tap brings them back.

From: HBO Max landscape player, simplified
5
Exit goes back, never out.

The ✕ returns to the child grid in portrait — it can never surface YouTube UI, the title is never a link, and there's no scrubbing past the wrapper.

Principle: two worlds, one wall
6
Back button + time-left pill frame the screen.

The ‹ back returns to the grid; the countdown pill keeps the daily limit visible during playback, so the break screen is expected, not sprung.

From: current LittleLoop build, kept

parent / pin & safety
1
Identity card first, Telegram-style.

Settings opens with who you are — avatar, name, one tap to account. Telegram, Cosmos and CVS all anchor settings with an identity header before any options.

From: Telegram · Cosmos · CVS Health
2
The child switcher is always visible.

Chips for Mia and Leo sit right under the header — every rule below is scoped to the selected child, and the "＋" chip is where the premium profile limit surfaces.

From: Netflix profile chip + Duolingo ABC add-a-child
3
Premium is the first card, Todoist-style.

Todoist leads settings with "Upgrade to Pro" and a plain one-line description. Same here — an honest sunny banner, not a popup.

From: Todoist "Upgrade to Pro"
4
Rows carry their value on the right.

"45 min ›", "7:30 PM ›", "On ›" — Cosmos and Todoist answer each row's question before it's tapped, turning the hub into a status page.

From: Cosmos · Todoist value labels
5
Limits are named presets, not a dial.

Duolingo, Py and Noom all set daily goals with a short named list — "45 min · Just right" beats a free stepper: faster to choose, and the names give each option a personality.

From: Duolingo · Py · Noom goal pickers
6
Safety states facts before controls.

TikTok's Family Pairing leads with plain sentences — "your teen's limit is 40 minutes; when it's reached, they can't continue without a passcode." LittleLoop's safety screen opens the same way: what's protected right now, then the levers.

From: TikTok Family Pairing / Screen Time Management
Premium

Premium appears exactly when a free limit is hit — the 16th video, the 3rd child — with the reason stated up top. Plain feature list, two prices, no tricks the parent will resent later.

parent / paywall
1
The trigger is the headline.

"Mia's playlist is full — 15 of 15" continues the honest counter from the playlist screen. The parent already saw this coming; the paywall just answers it.

From: honest limit counters (Parent HQ §06)
2
Four benefits, zero asterisks.

Plain checkmarks, "Everything in Free, forever" stated explicitly — parents of young kids are the most dark-pattern-wary audience there is.

Principle: trust, fast
3
"Not now" is a real exit.

Equal-weight dismiss link, no full-screen takeover on launch, never shown in Child Mode. Premium lives quietly in Settings until a cap makes it relevant.

Deliberate inversion of aggressive paywall patterns
